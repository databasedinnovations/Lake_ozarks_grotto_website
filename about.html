About Lake Ozarks Grotto

Dedicated to the exploration, study, and conservation of Missouri caves since 1967

Our History

Lake Ozarks Grotto was established in 1967, serving the Lake of the Ozarks region and central Missouri. For over five decades, our members have been dedicated to the exploration, study, and conservation of caves and karst features.

Throughout our history, we've been actively involved in cave mapping projects, contributing valuable data to the Missouri Speleological Survey. Our members have explored thousands of miles of underground passages, discovered new cave systems, and documented the unique geology and biology of Missouri's subterranean world.

We've also been leaders in cave conservation, organizing cleanup projects that have removed over 170 tons of trash from sinkholes and caves. Our educational programs have introduced countless individuals to the wonders of caving while emphasizing the importance of responsible exploration and environmental stewardship.

Today, Lake Ozarks Grotto continues to thrive as an active grotto, meeting monthly from April through November at Stark Caverns near Eldon, Missouri. We welcome both experienced cavers and those new to the sport who share our passion for underground exploration and conservation.
